# What Not To Build

What should you build today, and why? The first way to answer a "what should you do" question is to answer its logical opposite. It's much easier...

You should NOT:

- Build anything that ChatGPT or Claude can do out of the box
- Focus on building things with only publicly available data
- Think that vibe coding a frontend can differentiate your application
- Forget about the cycles of conversation turns and the role of memory in user experience
- Forget to add "agents" to your idea when selling it (even if it is simply using a simple agentic search or RAG tool)
- Cast aside _your specific industry expertise_
- Fail to plan for a positive feedback loop of usage -> data from usage -> better performance -> more usage -> more data -> better ...
- Recreate _any wheels_ from scratch that you can get off the shelf

# What Opus 4.6 Says
The real story of 2026 is the collision between agentic ambition and operational reality.

**The Agentic Wave Is Real ‚Äî But Meeting Friction**

Gartner predicts [40% of enterprise applications will feature task-specific AI agents by 2026](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-08-26-gartner-predicts-40-percent-of-enterprise-apps-will-feature-task-specific-ai-agents-by-2026-up-from-less-than-5-percent-in-2025), up from less than 5% in 2025. A [2026 Process Optimization Report](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260204723950/en/The-Enterprise-AI-Reality-Check-High-Ambitions-Meet-Operational-Barriers) surveying over 1,600 global business leaders found 85% of organizations want to be an "agentic enterprise" within three years, and 90% are already using or exploring multi-agent systems. Forrester predicts enterprise applications will [move beyond enabling employees with digital tools to accommodating a digital workforce of AI agents](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/predictions-2026-ai-agents-changing-business-models-and-workplace-culture-impact-enterprise-software/).

**But the ROI Reckoning Has Arrived**

MIT's *GenAI Divide* research found a [95% failure rate for enterprise generative AI projects](https://www.cio.com/article/4114010/2026-the-year-ai-roi-gets-real.html), defined as not having shown measurable financial returns within six months. Forrester predicts enterprises will [defer 25% of planned AI spending into 2027](https://medium.com/@Lisamedrouk/2026-ai-predictions-what-gartner-forrester-and-idc-reveal-for-tech-leaders-96cbe36b7985), as fewer than one-third currently link initiatives to tangible financial growth. Gartner predicts that [over 40% of agentic AI projects will be scrapped by 2027](https://www.kore.ai/blog/ai-agents-in-2026-from-hype-to-enterprise-reality) ‚Äî not because the models fail, but because organizations struggle to operationalize them.

**The Gap Between Ambition and Execution Is Stark**

Deloitte's 2025 Emerging Technology Trends study shows that while 30% of organizations are exploring agentic options and 38% are piloting solutions, [only 14% have solutions ready to deploy, and a mere 11% are actively using them in production](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends/2026/agentic-ai-strategy.html). Meanwhile, [76% of organizations admit their current processes are holding them back](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260204723950/en/The-Enterprise-AI-Reality-Check-High-Ambitions-Meet-Operational-Barriers), and 82% of decision-makers believe AI will fail to deliver ROI if it doesn't understand how the business actually runs.

**Governance Is Becoming Non-Negotiable**

Forrester predicts [60% of Fortune 100 companies will appoint a head of AI governance in 2026](https://www.ciodive.com/news/5-cio-predictions-for-ai-in-2026/807951/). Gartner's strategic predictions warn that ["death by AI" legal claims will exceed 2,000 by the end of 2026](https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/strategic-predictions-for-2026) due to insufficient risk guardrails. Adding urgency, the [EU AI Act reaches full application to high-risk systems in August 2026](https://www.joneswalker.com/en/insights/blogs/ai-law-blog/ten-ai-predictions-for-2026-what-leading-analysts-say-legal-teams-should-expect.html), with penalties reaching ‚Ç¨35 million or 7% of global revenue.

**Where Agents *Are* Delivering Value**

Agents are becoming mainstream in [constrained, well-governed domains such as IT operations, employee service, finance operations, onboarding, and support workflows](https://www.kore.ai/blog/ai-agents-in-2026-from-hype-to-enterprise-reality) ‚Äî environments that tolerate human-in-the-loop, have clear boundaries, and deliver fast ROI. At one global logistics company, [AI reduced a 30-to-40-person group down to four or five people](https://research.etr.ai/etr-data-drop/enterprise-ai-trends-2026-how-leaders-measure-roi-and-risk) by automating 80‚Äì90% of operational workflows.

**The Bottom Line**

The market is shifting from ["what can AI do?" to "what does AI cost, and can we prove its value?"](https://i10x.ai/news/ai-predictions-2026-shift-to-efficiency-governance). If 2025 was the year of realizing that generative AI has a value-realization problem, [2026 will be the year of doing something about it](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/five-trends-in-ai-and-data-science-for-2026/).
